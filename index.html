— The Pub

A proper local,
not a project.

Set on the corner of Stoop Lane and Station Road in Quorn, the Apple Tree is a 1930s building that has kept most of its bones — including the old off-sales hatch and the two small rooms that still divide bar from lounge.

The lounge has been gently brought up to date, with a wood burner that does most of the talking on a winter evening. Through the interconnecting door, the bar leans the other way: a TV showing the football, patio doors out to a front seating area, and a steady rhythm of regulars who have been propping it up for years.

We pour Draught Bass as the resident cask ale — a Premium Bitter at 4.4% — alongside a rotating handful of guest beers. Families and dogs (on the lead) are welcome in equal measure. The garden out the back is where the summer goes.

— The Garden

An orchard of an afternoon
at the back of Quorn.

The garden runs the length of the building — wide, sheltered, with a children’s play area at one end and tables enough for a Sunday. Dogs welcome on the lead. Bring the family. Bring the dog. Bring a paper.
