§ One · The pub

Ninety years on a quiet corner.

The Apple Tree was built in the thirties and has stood on the corner of Stoop Lane and Station Road ever since. Most of the original structure is still here — the off-sales hatch among it — though the inside was lifted gently into the present a short while ago. New paint, kept bones.

It’s a two-room pub with an interconnecting door, which means you can pick your mood: the lounge with the wood burner for a slow pint and a paper, or the bar for the match and a bit more company. Families welcome. Dogs welcome — on a lead, not the furniture.

The garden out the back is generous for a village pub. There’s a front patio too, where the patio doors of the bar open out for the warmer afternoons. Park up on the lane. Walk down if you’re local.
